Icelandic Geology

This page gives links to all the pages that I have been working on, mostly related to the geology of Iceland. These are just for fun, with some light research going in to a few of them.

The links below open new tabs to models and images that I have uploaded onto Github. This page will continue to be updated and improved as I learn more about html and CSS:


Hrutfjall

Hrutfjall is an impressive mountain in the south east of Iceland, forms part of the flank sequence of the Öræfajökull volcano. The link takes you to a panorama with geological interpretation information overlain. This mountain face formed to basis of a small project on the application of geological remote sensing to inaccessible areas of Iceland.

Early 1900's geological map of Iceland in 3D

This is a model of Icelandic geology as drawn by Thoroddsen 1901 overlain onto the Icelandic is50v DEM. This model was produced in QGIS using QGIS2threejs.

Spatial distribution map of variation in ΔPb208 in Icelandic rock samples

This is just that. A map of the ΔPb208 value of samples from the Georoc database and my own data. The map simply maps out where the greastest amount of plume influenced melt is being sampled as found at the surface. The deepest melting, therefore the lowest melt degree and highest proportion of plume material in found in Öræfajökull.
