Links on the left menu opens new tabs to models and images that I have uploaded onto Github. Below are descriptions of each link:


Hrutfjall, an impressive mountain in the south east of Iceland, forms part of the flank sequence of the Öræfajökull volcano. The link takes you to a panorama with geological interpretation information overlain. This mountain face formed to basis of a small project on the application of geological remote sensing to inaccessible areas of Iceland.

Íslandskort: This is a model of Icelandic geology as drawn by Thoroddsen 1901 overlain onto the Icelandic is50v DEM. This model was produced in QGIS using QGIS2threejs.

Spatial distribution map of variation in ΔPb208 in Icelandic basaltic rock samples, is just that. A map of the ΔPb208 value of samples from the Georoc database and my own data. The map simply maps out where the greastest amount of plume influenced melt is being sampled as found at the surface. The deepest melting, therefore the lowest melt degree and highest proportion of plume material in found in Öræfajökull.
